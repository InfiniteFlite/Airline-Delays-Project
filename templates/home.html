I have chosen data about airline delays across the US. It covers many reasons including: weather, security, airline issues, and more. The data can be found here

This data is about delays across numerous different airlines and airports. It goes from January of 2003 to January of 2016.
The 29 largest airports in the US are represented. These 29 airports had a combined 580 million passengers through them in 2016, that is 70% of annual US airline traffic. (Source)

Depending on the year, each airport had between 7 and 15 different airlines report their numbers. This means that the totals may vary from year to year. I can account for these differences by using percents of data instead of the whole numbers, as these will vary depending on the number of airlines involved.
